Funebra Dynamic Math Objects
A closer look at life in a simulation — by Peter M. Lugha

We took a closer look at life inside a simulation. Our software — capable of zooming in and out from science-fiction to science-facts and all the way into pure imagination — led us to a new category of digital materials we now call Dynamic Math Objects.

These objects form the core of Funebra Math research, developed and continually explored by the owner and co-founder of pLabs Entertainment, Peter M. Lugha.

Although we do not yet possess full control or complete scientific understanding of this mathematical–biological domain, the research continues to reveal valuable insights — from simple, single-battery-level origins to the complexities of quantum physics that structure our universe.

We have discovered possibilities for interacting with DNA digitally, including early opportunities to recode it inside virtual space. This contributes to the conclusion that everything is possible — and therefore even nothingness is a valid possibility.
